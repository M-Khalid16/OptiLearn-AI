# OptiLearn AI Demo Script

Opening: Students today can access more information than any previous generation, but information alone does not create engineering understanding. OptiLearn AI helps learners move from reading an equation to questioning it, changing its parameters, visualizing its consequences, and testing what they understand.

## 3-to-5-Minute Flow

1. **Home** — Introduce the platform as an educational digital twin for optical communication. Emphasize the movement from information access to active engineering reasoning.
2. **Digital Twin: fiber attenuation** — Set launch power, fiber length, and attenuation. Show received power and explain how dB loss maps to power ratio.
3. **Digital Twin: chromatic dispersion** — Switch to dispersion. Adjust bit rate, linewidth, distance, and dispersion coefficient. Highlight pulse broadening and the educational Gaussian approximation.
4. **Digital Twin: FSO** — Switch to free-space optical communication. Demonstrate beam spreading, receiver aperture capture, pointing loss, and atmospheric attenuation.
5. **Mode Explorer** — Show scalar LP modes, V-number, mode fields, Gaussian launch coupling, meridional rays, and skew rays. Clarify that modes and rays are complementary educational models.
6. **Quiz Lab** — Start a short formative quiz and answer one question to show local deterministic grading.
7. **Grounded AI Tutor** — Upload a text-based PDF in Lecture Notes, then ask a question in AI Tutor. Point out page-level evidence. If API access is unavailable, show the fallback message: live AI explanation is unavailable, while deterministic simulations and evidence remain available. In demo mode, explain that local templates are not live OpenAI responses.

Closing: OptiLearn AI is designed to help future engineers build not only knowledge, but also visual intuition, critical judgement, confidence, and lifelong-learning habits—the foundations they will need to contribute to the next generation of communication, photonic, and quantum technologies.
